British citizen living in beautiful British Columbia.

As a social scientist by training, researcher by profession and data nerd by accident, I'm facinated by complex socio-technical systems, the way people cooperate and the techniques we can use to understand them.

Educationally and professionally, I've jumped around a bit. During my undergrad, I switched from stdying aeronautical engineering to politics, discovering along way that I actually (really) enjoyed statistics. I fell into the world of data while working at the Open Data Institute, uncovering worrying levels of excitement for some seemingly dry topics (data standards, yay!). I spent four years at the ODI doing research, giving talks and providing advice to businesses and governments about how (carefully) increasing access to data can bring about great benefits to society.

Being surrounded by data experts and other clever people doing exciting things with data, I felt inspired to improve my own technical and policy skills by going back to university. In 2018, I enrolled in Oxford University's MSc Social Science of the Internet program. Based in the world-leading, multidisciplinary Oxford Internet Institute, I mainly focused on improving my skills in computational social science methods, including courses in social network analysis, agent-based modelling, accessing data, big data analytics etc.

Based on my work at the ODI, I became enthralled with the concept of peer-production, where individuals widely distributed around the world come together to produce a collective, openly available output without any direct financial incentive. Because I find these systems so fascinating, during the degree I focused on the OpenStreetMap project in many of my papers and my thesis (some of which are detailed on this site), leading one professor to joke that I have an MSc in OpenStreetMap studies.

In my spare time I like to ski and hike (mandatory for BC residents), play boardgames (mandatory for millennial nerds) and drink overly pricey craft beers (madatory for ex-East London cultural hipsters). On occassion I also dabble in photography and play around with wire sculptures.
